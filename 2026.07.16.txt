

A descendant of the XFG.1.1 lineage, specifically the strain carrying 17 spike mutations relative to JN.1, continues to grow rapidly. It is hoped that this strain will receive official designation soon.


XHA and XHB have emerged as new lineages with transmission potential, and both deserve close attention.



PQ.16.1.1 continues to expand in Asia. Notably, the growth rate of SV.1 has suddenly accelerated, suggesting that its descendants may have the potential to challenge SV.2.1. Indeed, more descendants of SV.1 are rapidly emerging.


The recently designated SV.1.2 lineage, carrying the S:K679R mutation, could also be one of the challengers.



The TA.1 lineage continues to grow rapidly in regions or countries with low sequencing coverage, and currently warrants continued monitoring.


In the United States, RW.1.1, a descendant branch of the XFJ lineage, continues to grow. The S:V1264L mutation is also appearing convergently in the RF.5 lineage, and this mutation can also be observed in some recent branches of XFG
