



Since the beginning of 2026, strains of the XFG.1.1 lineage have been prevalent in the United States, persisting to the present. Currently, various SARS-CoV-2 variants are dominating distinct regions and nations.


Now the situation seems to be shifting once more. Currently, the majority of the dominant strains are confined to just a few lineages: XFG, PE.1, NB.1.8.1, XFJ, BA.3.2.2, and LF.7.9!


Information regarding the PE.1 lineage will not be elaborated upon further. For the other families, a detailed explanation of their primary sub-variants will follow.



Let’s start with the NB.1.8.1 lineage, which circulated in Asia and several other regions last year. At present, certain emerging sub-lineages are spreading. Among them, the dominant ones primarily include the PQ.2.8.1, PQ.2.1.11, PQ.17, and PQ.16.1.1 strains!


The PQ.17, PQ.2.1.11, and PQ.16.1.1 strains uniformly possess a core spike protein mutation, S:I478T. Furthermore, the RK.1 branch of the PQ.17 lineage and the PQ.16.1.1 strain both harbor a newly identified spike protein mutation, S:D420N. The prevalence of this mutation among other branches within specific regions is on par with that of the S:I478T and S:T572I mutations.


The NB.1.8.1 strain harbors a series of mutations, namely S:T22N, S:F59S, S:G184S, S:A435S, S:F456L, S:K478I, and S:Q493E!


The PQ.2.1.11 strain harbors a series of mutations, namely S:T22N, S:F59S, S:G184S, S:A435S, S:F456L, S:I478T, S:Q493E, S:S680P, and S:H954Y!



The PQ.2.8.1 strain acquired the S:H445P mutation on the basis of the NB.1.8.1 lineage. Additionally, the RC.1 strain, which demonstrates notable fitness and greater transmission potential within the PQ.2.8.1 family, has also acquired the S:I478T mutation. In summary, the RC.1 strain harbors the mutations S:T22N, S:F59S, S:G184S, S:A435S, S:H445P, S:F456L, S:I478T, and S:Q493E. It is currently circulating in nations and regions including Japan!

Meanwhile, in distinct regions, another strain designated PQ.16.1.1 has become prevalent, having emerged driven by the S:I478T and S:D420N mutations. Furthermore, in some areas, the S:D420N mutation has been detected across multiple other related and analogous branches. The SV.1.1 strain represents a currently monitored descendant of the PQ.16.1.1 lineage, harboring mutations S:T22N, S:F59S, S:G184S, S:D253G, S:N417T, S:D420N, S:A435S, S:F456L, S:I478T, S:Q493E, and S:Q677H.


Likewise, the RK.1 strain within the PQ.17 family also possesses the S:D420N mutation.
Currently, the RK.1 and PQ.17.11.3 strains are likely the two most promising branches within the PQ.17 family!
The RK.1 strain carries mutations including S:T22N, S:F59S, S:G184S, S:D420N, S:A435S, S:H445P, S:F456L, S:I478T, and S:Q493E.
In contrast, the PQ.17.11.3 strain features the S:T22N, S:F59S, S:G184S, S:F186L, S:A435S, S:F456L, S:I478T, S:Q493E, and S:T572I mutations.



In addition to the strains mentioned above, the PQ.4.10.1 strain also deserves attention. It has acquired an extra S:V1264L mutation on top of the NB.1.8.1 strain. Furthermore, its descendant, the RY.1.1 strain, gained the S:I478T mutation on this basis. The RY.1.1 strain carries the following mutations: S:T22N, S:F59S, S:G184S, S:A435S, S:F456L, S:I478T, S:Q493E, and S:V1264L! While RY.1.1 might not necessarily challenge other strains within the NB.1.8.1 family, the addition of two key mutations—S:I478T and S:V1264L—still means it warrants our attention!


Now, let’s look at the XFG lineage. XFG strains carry mutations including S:T22N, S:S31P, S:K182R, S:R190S, S:R346T, S:K444R, S:H445R, S:F456L, S:N487D, S:Q493E, and S:T572I. We are currently in a phase where XFG.1.1 is replacing the original XFG. The main potential competitors that could challenge the XFG.1.1 family right now are the RV.1 strain and the XFG.6.3.3 strain!

As the RV.1 and XFG.6.3.3 strains challenge XFG.1.1, a new potential sub-lineage has been detected within the monitored XFG.1.1 strain. This new, currently unassigned branch carries the following mutations: S:T22N, S:S31P, S:E96D, S:K182R, S:R190S, S:A222V, S:D253G, S:R346T, S:K444R, S:R445P, S:W452R, S:F456L, S:N487D, S:Q493E, S:T572I, S:S680F, and S:S704L!


S:A222V was one of the relatively early convergent mutations in SARS-CoV-2. S:D253G is a key defining mutation for XBB.2, and is also a defining mutation for the PQ.16.1 strain, which facilitated the international spread of PQ.16.1.1. While the combined effect of S:I478T and S:D420N plays the dominant role in PQ.16.1.1, the contribution of S:D253G should not be underestimated! S:S680F, S:S680P，
and S:S704L are frequently observed convergent mutations within the XFG family. Notably, S:S704L played a critical role in the BA.2.12.1 strain and also drove significant evolutionary developments during the XBB era!


Ever since XFG.1.1 overtook XFG in proportion, RV.1 and the XFG.6 lineage have become the most likely potential challengers to XFG.1.1's dominance. Within the XFG.6 lineage, the XFG.6.3.3 strain stands out as the most competitive challenger!
RV.1 is a descendant of XFG.23.1.3. Compared to the broader XFG family, RV.1 has acquired additional mutations: S:L335F, S:A1174V, and S:S446N. The S:L335F and S:S446N mutations play a critical role, particularly S:S446N, which is also found in XFG.6.3.3 and other XFG descendants!


RV.1 has shown strong growth in both Africa and the United States!


The RV.1 strain carries mutations including S:T22N, S:S31P, S:K182R, S:R190S, S:L335F, S:R346T, S:K444R, S:H445R, S:S446N, S:F456L, S:N487D, S:Q493E, S:T572I, and S:A1174V!



In addition to RV.1, the XFG.6.3.3 strain has also acquired the S:S446N mutation and is showing growth in certain regions. A further descendant of XFG.6.3.3, the SW.1 strain, has gained the S:A435S mutation. With this addition, the SW.1 strain carries mutations including S:T22N, S:S31P, S:K182R, S:R190S, S:R346T, S:A435S, S:L441I, S:K444R, S:H445R, S:S446N, S:F456L, S:N487D, S:Q493E, and S:T572I!


In addition to the RV.1 and SW.1 strains, there are other strains with some potential, though likely without causing a major impact, such as XFG.10.4.1. The XFG.10.4.1 strain has acquired additional S:A435S and S:R445P mutations; in other words, it contains S:T22N, S:S31P, S:K182R, S:R190S, S:R346T, S:A435S, S:K444R, S:R445P, S:F456L, S:N487D, S:Q493E, and S:T572I. Although this strain may not necessarily have the capacity to take off, it does, after all, possess two new spike protein mutations!



The RF.5 and RW.1.1 strains are showing rapid growth in certain countries and regions. RW.1.1 is a sub-lineage of the XFJ.3.1.2 family, but its mutation profile is very similar to that of RF.5. This is precisely why RW.1.1 has also been placed on the watch list!


The RF.5 strain is a sub-lineage of PY.1.1.1, which itself belongs to the LF.7.9 lineage! PY.1.1.1 showed rapid growth in Asia early on, but was confined to only a few countries and regions. Now, however, its descendant, RF.5, is making a resurgence. The PY.1.1.1 strain carries mutations including S:T22N, S:S31P, S:K182R, S:R190S, S:R346T, S:A435S, S:L441R, S:K444R, S:H445P, S:F456L, S:A475V, and S:K679R!



The RW.1.1 strain also holds a notable proportion. Although it does not belong to the PY.1.1.1 lineage, the XFJ lineage (to which RW.1.1 belongs) shares significant mutational similarity with PY.1.1.1. These similarities lead to frequent comparisons with the PY.1.1.1 lineage when these strains are discussed. RW.1.1 is a descendant of XFJ.3.1.2. Compared to the broader XFJ lineage, RW.1.1 has acquired extra mutations: S:I19V, S:A435S, S:K478T, and S:V1264L.



The RF.5 strain carries mutations including S:T22N, S:S31P, S:M153I, S:K182R, S:R190S, S:R346T, S:A435S, S:L441R, S:K444R, S:H445P, S:F456L, S:A475V, S:K478T, and S:K679R!
