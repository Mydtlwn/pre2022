



Since the beginning of 2026, strains of the XFG.1.1 lineage have been prevalent in the United States, persisting to the present. Currently, various SARS-CoV-2 variants are dominating distinct regions and nations.


Now the situation seems to be shifting once more. Currently, the majority of the dominant strains are confined to just a few lineages: XFG, PE.1, NB.1.8.1, XFJ, BA.3.2.2, and LF.7.9!


Information regarding the PE.1 lineage will not be elaborated upon further. For the other families, a detailed explanation of their primary sub-variants will follow.



Let’s start with the NB.1.8.1 lineage, which circulated in Asia and several other regions last year. At present, certain emerging sub-lineages are spreading. Among them, the dominant ones primarily include the PQ.2.8.1, PQ.2.1.11, PQ.17, and PQ.16.1.1 strains!


The PQ.17, PQ.2.1.11, and PQ.16.1.1 strains uniformly possess a core spike protein mutation, S:I478T. Furthermore, the RK.1 branch of the PQ.17 lineage and the PQ.16.1.1 strain both harbor a newly identified spike protein mutation, S:D420N. The prevalence of this mutation among other branches within specific regions is on par with that of the S:I478T and S:T572I mutations.
