



Recently, a new potential ‘dark horse’ has emerged: the TA.1 lineage, a descendant of XFG.14.8. More precisely, the dark horse is a sub-branch of TA.1, which has acquired the S:S446N, S:N532S, S:Q613H, S:V642G, and S:K679R mutations on top of the XFG.14.8 background. Relative to the JN.1 strain, this lineage possesses 18 spike protein mutations,  a number comparable to another previously noted branch of XFG.1.1. Notably, the S:S446N mutation has appeared across various branches of the XFG family; the S:Q613H mutation was previously seen in descendant lineages of CH.1.1; and the S:K679R mutation previously converged in LP.8.1, and has also been acquired convergently in existing XFG strains.


Recently, the PQ.16.1.1 lineage has continued to circulate in Asia, primarily driven by the SV.2 and SV.2.1 sub-lineages. However, the SV.1 lineage has now spawned numerous distinct branches. One of these branches has acquired the S:S446N mutation, and this descendant of SV.1 is exhibiting mutations that are highly convergent with those seen in the XFG family. While it remains uncertain whether this will yield the same effect in the NB.1.8.1 lineage, it is still a cause for concern. This is similar to how the XFG family has seen the emergence of many variants carrying the S:A435S mutation.
